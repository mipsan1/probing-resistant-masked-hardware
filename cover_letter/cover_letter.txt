Dear Editor,

We are pleased to submit our manuscript entitled "Probing-Resistant
Masked Hardware: Formal Security Proofs and Simulation-Based
Side-Channel Verification" for consideration in IEEE Transactions
on Information Forensics and Security.

The manuscript presents a first-order Boolean-masked AES-128
hardware implementation whose side-channel resistance is (i)
formally proven in the robust (glitch-extended) d-probing model
and (ii) empirically verified through three independent
simulation-based verification channels:

  1. A Python re-implementation of the PROLEAD robust d-probing
     statistical verifier, applied to the Yosys gate-level
     netlist.  All DFF Q wires pass the d=1 single-wire
     I(W;S) < 0.05 bit threshold, and all 2-wire combinations
     pass the I(W_i,W_j;S) < 0.10 bit threshold.

  2. A Test Vector Leakage Assessment (TVLA) on a 10,000-triple
     Hamming-distance power trace generated by gate-level
     simulation.  The maximum |t| statistic observed is 2.0244
     for d=1 and 1.0000 for d=2, well below the |t|=4.5
     industry threshold at 99.999% confidence.

  3. A direct cross-check with the public PROLEAD C++ toolchain
     (Müller & Moradi, TCHES 2022) using 1,536,000 Monte-Carlo
     simulations per probe set.  The PROLEAD config and
     matching custom Yosys cell library are pinned in the
     public artifact repository for every push:

       https://github.com/mipsan1/probing-resistant-masked-hardware

All three channels agree on the absence of d-probing leakage for
both the first-order and second-order designs, and the complete
source code, synthesis scripts, and CI artifacts are released as
open source at the URL above.

We believe this work is of interest to the TIFS readership
because it demonstrates a full open-source verification chain for
masked hardware — from formal d-probing security proofs to
gate-level statistical leakage assessment to independent
third-party tool cross-validation — thereby lowering the bar
for third-party reproducibility in the masked-hardware
verification community.

The manuscript is 8 pages, including all figures, tables, and
references, in accordance with the TIFS submission guidelines.
The source files (manuscript.tex, manuscript.bib, IEEEtran.cls)
are included for the IEEE typesetting process.

We confirm that this manuscript has not been published
elsewhere and is not under simultaneous consideration by any
other journal or conference.  All co-authors (none, single-author
manuscript) have approved this submission.
